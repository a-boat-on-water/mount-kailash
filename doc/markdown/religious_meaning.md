# The Religious Meaning of Mount Kailash

冈仁波齐是本教（苯教）、印度教、耆那教、藏传佛教四个古老宗教共同认定的圣山，藏语名意为「珍贵的雪山」。在藏地众多神山中位居其首，具有无上的宗教地位。

*Mount Kailash is the sacred mountain jointly venerated by four ancient religions: Bon, Hinduism, Jainism, and Tibetan Buddhism. Its Tibetan name means 'precious snow mountain'. It holds the highest religious status among all sacred mountains of Tibet.*

## Names & Etymology

- **冈仁波齐** (Chinese) — 珍贵的雪山 — Precious Snow Mountain
- **གངས་རིན་པོ་ཆེ།** (Tibetan) — 珍贵的雪山
- **ཏི་སེ། / གངས་ཏི་སེ།** (Tibetan (classical)) — 大雪山（藏梵合成词）
- **Kelāsa** (Sanskrit) — 水晶 (crystal) — Crystal (the mountain's shape resembles a crystal stupa)
- **Kailash / Kailas** (English)

## Four Religions That Venerate Mount Kailash

### 本教（苯教） (Bön)
- **Native to Tibet:** Yes
- **Circumambulation direction:** counter-clockwise (逆时针/反方向)
- **Significance (中文):** 西藏本土宗教，发源于冈仁波齐周边。冈仁波齐是其「三界宇宙观」的中心：山尖通上界（拉界，天界），山底深入下界（鲁界，地底/水中），中界为念界（人间）——贯通宇宙三界。
- **Significance (English):** Native religion of Tibet, originated near Mt. Kailash. The mountain is the axis of the Bön three-realm cosmology: its peak reaches the upper realm (heaven), its base extends into the lower realm (underworld/water), with the human realm in between.

### 印度教 (Hinduism)
- **Native to Tibet:** No
- **Circumambulation direction:** clockwise (顺时针)
- **Significance (中文):** 主神 Shiva（湿婆）的居所 Kailash 山即冈仁波齐峰，他与妻子 Parvati（帕尔瓦蒂）及孩子们住在这里。
- **Significance (English):** Considered the abode of Lord Shiva, who dwells here with his consort Parvati and their children.

### 耆那教 (Jainism)
- **Native to Tibet:** No
- **Circumambulation direction:** clockwise (顺时针)
- **Significance (中文):** 其初祖 Rishabhadeva（里沙布得瓦）在冈仁波齐前面的 Ashtapad（意为「八层」）山苦修多年后得道，也有说法称 Ashtapad 就是冈仁波齐峰。
- **Significance (English):** Their first Tirthankara, Rishabhadeva, attained enlightenment after long austerities on Mt. Ashtapada (meaning 'eight steps') near Kailash; some traditions identify Ashtapada with Kailash itself.

### 藏传佛教 (Tibetan Buddhism)
- **Native to Tibet:** Yes
- **Circumambulation direction:** clockwise (顺时针)
- **Significance (中文):** 冈仁波齐是密宗五大本尊之一「胜乐金刚」（Chakrasamvara）的居所，是其身、语、意三坛城之「身坛城」。语坛城为拉契神山，意坛城为扎日神山。
- **Significance (English):** Mt. Kailash is the abode of Chakrasamvara, one of the five great tantric deities. It is his 'body mandala' (the corresponding speech mandala is Lapchi, mind mandala is Tsari).

## Cosmic Axis: Mt. Meru / Sumeru

须弥山（Sumeru / Meru）是印度教、耆那教、佛教共有的宇宙论中心。一些学者根据典籍中对须弥山特征的描述，认为其在现实世界对应的就是冈仁波齐峰。

*Mount Meru (Sumeru) is the cosmic axis shared by Hindu, Jain, and Buddhist cosmologies. Many scholars identify Mount Kailash as the real-world correspondent of Meru.*

> **Important caveat:** 所谓冈仁波齐是「宇宙（世界）中心」指的其实是「各宗教宇宙观的中心」，勿将其套入现代天文学、地理学的「宇宙/地球中心」概念。

## The Legend of the Four Great Rivers

传统文化中冈仁波齐「嘴涌四河」（ཁ་འབབ་བཞི།）：象泉河、孔雀河、马泉河、狮泉河四条大河，先在地底流淌很远，再分别从西面大象嘴、南面孔雀嘴、东面马嘴、北面狮嘴四个方位涌出地面。

*Traditional teaching of the 'Four Rivers from Four Mouths': the Elephant, Peacock, Horse, and Lion rivers flow underground from Kailash and emerge from four directional 'mouths' (elephant west, peacock south, horse east, lion north).*

**Scientific note:** 从水文学角度，这四条河发源于冈仁波齐「周边」，最近的象泉河源头距冈仁波齐峰约36公里，最远的马泉河源头约137公里。它们的源头跟冈仁波齐的冰雪融化没有直接关系。

| Direction | Mouth | River | Flows to |
|---|---|---|---|
| 西/West | 大象嘴 | 象泉河 | 印度河 (downstream) |
| 南/South | 孔雀嘴 | 孔雀河 | 恒河 (as tributary) |
| 东/East | 马嘴 | 马泉河 | 雅鲁藏布江/布拉马普特拉河 (true source) |
| 北/North | 狮嘴 | 狮泉河 | 印度河 (true source) |

## Key Legends

### 米拉日巴与那若本琼斗法争夺冈仁波齐
*Milarepa vs Naro Bönchung — Contest for Mt. Kailash — tradition: 藏传佛教（噶举派）vs 本教*

藏传佛教噶举派大师米拉日巴（1040–1123 或 1052–1135）与本教的那若本琼约定，谁先于某日清晨登上冈仁波齐山顶，神山就归谁。那若本琼凌晨骑着法鼓向山顶飞去，但米拉日巴的咒语令他只能绕山腰打转。日出时分，米拉日巴抖衣袖一飞而上山顶，刚好接住第一缕阳光。冈仁波齐南壁中间那条由上至下的长长槽壑，相传就是那若本琼跌落时划出的痕迹。

*English:* The Buddhist master Milarepa and the Bön master Naro Bönchung agreed to race to Kailash's summit at dawn — the winner would claim the mountain. Naro flew up on his magic drum at midnight, but Milarepa's mantra trapped him circling at mid-mountain. At first light, Milarepa rose instantly to the peak. Naro fell off, drum and all — the long vertical groove on Kailash's south face is said to be the mark of his fall.

> **Scholarly note:** 也有学者指出，历史上并不存在那若本琼这个人；早期的《米拉日巴传》中并没有此斗法情节，认为这些故事只是后人因应宗教竞争演绎而出。

### 郭仓巴开创外转经道
*Götsangpa opens the outer kora route — tradition: 藏传佛教 噶举派 竹巴噶举上竹巴支派*

现今的转山线路相传是由噶举派大师、竹巴噶举上竹巴支派创始人郭仓巴（གོ་ཚང་པ།，1189–1258）于公元十三世纪初探索开创的。八百多年来已有无数朝圣者走过。

*English:* The outer kora route around Mt. Kailash is traditionally said to have been pioneered by Götsangpa (1189–1258), founder of the Upper Drukpa Kagyu sub-school, in the early 13th century. Pilgrims have walked it for over 800 years.

## Why No One Climbs Mt. Kailash

冈仁波齐神山从未有「凡人」登顶的记录。1985 年意大利登山家梅斯纳尔获得登山许可，但出于对宗教圣山的敬畏主动放弃，改为转山两圈。2001 年西班牙登山家诺瓦斯也因国际抗议放弃登山计划。梅斯纳尔说：「如果我们征服了这座山，那我们就征服了其他人灵魂中的一些东西。」

*No human has ever climbed Mt. Kailash's summit. In 1985, the Italian mountaineer Reinhold Messner was granted a climbing permit but voluntarily declined out of religious respect, doing two koras instead. In 2001, Spanish climber Jesús Martínez Novas also abandoned his climb after international protest. Messner: 'If we conquer this mountain, then we conquer something in other people's souls.'*

## Horse Year — Special Significance

藏地有说法称释迦牟尼诞生、成道都是在马年，并相传佛祖曾到过冈仁波齐。每逢马年，十方诸佛菩萨、人、天、非天都聚集在冈仁波齐周围，非常殊胜，所以马年转山活动特别隆重热闹。「马年转一圈冈仁波齐等于平常年份的 13 圈」——指的是功德相当于 13 倍。

*Tibetan tradition holds that the Buddha was born and attained enlightenment in horse years, and that he visited Kailash. In horse years, all buddhas, devas, and beings gather at Kailash, making pilgrimage especially auspicious. One kora during a horse year is said to be worth thirteen ordinary koras — meaning 13× the merit, not that one such circuit equals 13 separate koras for purposes of qualifying for the inner route.*

> 冈仁波齐芝热寺住持丁增南江仁波切澄清：「马年一圈相当于往年 13 圈」指的是功德相当于 13 倍。如果要转内转，还是得先真真正正转满 13 次外转。
